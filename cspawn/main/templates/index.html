The Phantom Server

In the gloom of his solitary apartment, lit only by the dim glow of an aging monitor, Elias Marlowe pondered the cryptic email he had received. A single command: telnet 192.168.13.13 6666. No sender, no subject. A jest? A lure? Or something far darker?

His fingers, trembling with a nameless trepidation, entered the command. The terminal flickered, then dissolved into a void of silence. A connection was established, and from the depths of that digital abyss, a message materialized:

"Welcome, Elias. We have been expecting you."

His breath caught. The server knew his name. Impossible! And yet, before he could react, the cursor moved—unbidden—scrolling through commands he had never typed.

“WHO ARE YOU?” he attempted to reply, his keystrokes sluggish as though wading through thick, unseen molasses.

The response was instant.

"We are the ghosts in the machine, Elias. We know what you seek."

He felt the room grow colder, his breath now visible in the pale light. A file named TRUTH.txt appeared in his directory. Hesitant, yet unable to resist, he opened it.

It contained but a single sentence.

“You should not have logged in.”

At that moment, the lights flickered. The screen went black. And somewhere, in the unseen recesses of cyberspace, something laughed.
